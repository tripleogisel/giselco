iA Collaborative & FedEx, 2018

Led UX / Ui redesigns to FedEx's mobile web design language, "1DX" over the course of a 1 month contract. We made several improvements to the Design Language – this case study covers our most important improvement, the Narratives Framework.

The Challenge

Bring a new level of UX design to improve the Fed Ex Digital design language

My Role

As Senior UX Designer on this project I led the entire effort, managed its timelines, and delivered written guidelines and visual assets for the design language.

Tools Used

Keynote, Principle for Mac, Sketch

The Challenge

iA collaborative has worked with FedEx as a design team and general consultant for over 10 years. In 2015, iA built a design language called "1DX" to support FedEx's design teams with a common source of truth when designing for fedex.com

In May 2018, iA asked me to help improve the FedEx Design Language.

The Approach

We began work by understanding how the current design system did not meet the needs of designers and engineers at FedEx. FedEx handed over specific questions / complaints from their designers about where they felt the design language fell short. In context of these considerations, We audited the existing dot-com experience to understand how these challenges manifested themselves in the product.

The biggest win

While many of the designers' complaints described very granular issues, our audit led us to uncovered an underlying structural problem. The guidelines had great typography and button rules, but didn't offer suggestions for how to combine elements to tell stories on a page. This resulted in pages that lacked a primary call to action. We developed a concept called the Narratives Framework address this structural challenge at scale. Page narratives maximize readability, vertical rhythm, and responsiveness across devices. Use page narratives as a template to organize pages into digestible story, including a single most important Main Narrative at the top.
