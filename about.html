I'm Will Gisel and I started Gisel & Company, LLC. My design career began as the lone customer support rep for a small start up. This experience working with frustrated users and solving problems provided me with an invaluable design foundation built on a deep appreciation for listening to users.

As I transitioned into being a full time designer, working at a small start up also gave me the opportunity to work very closely with our product and engineering teams. This intimate exposure helped me develop a keen sense of how to make design thrive as a function within the broader organization. For example, I learned how to match my process to the pace of the organization on a given project. Need a fully functioning prototype to pitch a new product tomorrow? I can do that. Need to redesign the entire product and ship it in 6 months? I can do that, too.

When doing the latter, I follow a design process that follows the user above all else, separates architecture from visual design, and prioritizes defining problems clearly before considering the solutions. This delivers intuitive products that scale.
